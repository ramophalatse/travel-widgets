Nagasaki — Peace Museum + Glover Garden + Cathedral
FREE tours
Morning
NGS-005 Best of Nagasaki — FREE, 4hrs, moderate
Atomic Bomb Museum: among the world's most candid and moving museums. Clock stopped at 11:02am on 9 August 1945. Peace Memorial Park near the hypocentre. Dejima: the Dutch trading post — Japan's only connection to the outside world during 200 years of Edo-period isolation.
RSSC FREE tour
Early afternoon
Glover Garden (shuttle/taxi, ~10 min)
Thomas Glover's hillside estate. 19th-century foreign merchant mansions with harbour panorama. Glover was the Scottish merchant who inspired the setting for Madame Butterfly. ~90 min walk.
Mid afternoon
Oura Cathedral + Hamanomachi arcade
Oura Cathedral (5 min from Glover): Japan's only pre-Meiji Gothic church (UNESCO, built 1864) — the site where hidden Christians revealed themselves after 250 years of underground practice. Then Hamanomachi shopping arcade: Arita/Hasami ceramics, Fukusaya Castella cake (est. 1624 — a Portuguese-origin sponge cake, Nagasaki's most famous export).
Late afternoon
Shikairo restaurant — champon noodles
The 130-year-old restaurant that invented champon: a rich pork-bone broth loaded with seafood, pork, and vegetables — thick and deeply satisfying. Also order sara udon (crispy noodles with the same toppings). Not to be missed.
~ZAR 2,000 (transport + shopping + dinner)

Sun 22 Apr · Day 19
Hiroshima — Peace Museum + Miyajima Island ⭐
Most significant day
The most emotionally significant port of the entire itinerary. Two world-class experiences with completely different energy: weight and beauty. Cherry blossom likely still present in Peace Park (late sakura season).
Morning
Peace Memorial Museum — independent self-guided
Take FREE shuttle to city, then tram ¥180pp to Peace Memorial Park. Do this without a tour guide — this museum demands your own pace. Sequence: Children's Peace Monument (Sadako and the thousand cranes) → Cenotaph → Flame of Peace (aligned through arch to the Dome) → Atomic Bomb Dome exterior (left standing exactly as it fell) → Museum interior: 90 min minimum. Victims' personal belongings, watch stopped at 8:15am, shadow burned permanently into stone steps. Entry ¥200pp. Come out before noon.
¥200pp entry — self-guided
Afternoon
HIR-003 Miyajima Island — FREE RSSC, 4.25hrs
RSSC handles JR train + ferry logistics to Miyajima (UNESCO World Heritage). Floating Itsukushima Shrine (13th century, built over water — the great torii gate rises from the sea at high tide). Free-roaming Shika deer that have lived alongside the shrine for centuries. Omotesando shopping arcade. If time: Daishoin Temple above the shrine — far less crowded, hauntingly atmospheric mountain Buddhist temple.
RSSC FREE tour — book separately from museum morning
Evening
Hiroshima-style okonomiyaki dinner
Okonomiyaki-mura (Okonomiyaki Village): 6-storey building, 25+ stalls, each with their own recipe. Hiroshima style is layered (noodles + egg + cabbage + pork + batter) — completely distinct from Osaka-style. Any stall is excellent. Order the soba-iri (with noodles) version.
Miyajima souvenir: momiji manju — maple leaf-shaped cakes, warm, freshly baked (sweet bean or custard). Buy from a bakery with a visible oven, not a packet. Also: Miyajima lacquerware shamoji (rice paddles) are the island's iconic craft.
~ZAR 1,500 (tram + museum + dinner)

Check in to hotel (~15:00). Light walk to Shinjuku Gyoen (national garden, stunning in afternoon light, ¥500 entry, closes 18:30). Or simply: Kabukichō area and the tiny Golden Gai drinking lanes — six narrow alleys, ~200 micro-bars, each with 5-8 seats.
Evening
Omoide Yokocho ("Memory Lane") dinner
Under the train tracks, steps from Shinjuku West Exit — a warren of tiny smoky yakitori stalls. The perfect low-key first Tokyo evening. Skewered chicken, pork belly, gizzards over charcoal. Asahi draught.
~ZAR 2,500 (transfer + dinner)
Sun 28 Apr · Tokyo Day 1
Asakusa · Senso-ji · Ueno · Yanaka
East Tokyo
Get a Suica IC card at Shinjuku or Yokohama station — used for trains, metro, buses, and convenience stores across Tokyo.
Early
Senso-ji Temple, Asakusa
Tokyo's oldest Buddhist temple (7th century). Go early before the crowds. Walk the Nakamise-dori shopping street for omiyage gifts, fans, and ceramics. The Thunder Gate (Kaminarimon) at dawn is worth the early start.
Mid-morning
Ueno Park + Tokyo National Museum
Japan's finest collection of samurai armour, ceramics, lacquerware, Buddhist sculpture. Visit today (Sunday open) rather than tomorrow when some sections may be busier. Ueno Park also has Zoo and multiple museum buildings.
Afternoon
Yanaka neighbourhood
Old Edo-era neighbourhood that survived WWII bombing intact. Traditional shotengai (covered shopping street), small temples, ancient cemetery with old cherry trees, artisan shops selling handmade ceramics and textiles. One of the most atmospheric corners of Tokyo. Fez: independent ceramics and craft shops here are exceptional.
Evening
Isetan Shinjuku basement food hall
Return to Shinjuku. Isetan B2: Japan's finest department store food basement — artisan wagashi, premium sushi, bento sets, cheese, wine, pastry. Extraordinary as a dinner solution or just to browse. Or: tonkatsu at Maisen Shinjuku (celebrated pork cutlet restaurant).
~ZAR 3,000 (museum + transport + dinner)
Mon 29 Apr · Tokyo Day 2 — Shōwa Day Holiday
Meiji Shrine · Harajuku · Omotesando · Shibuya
Public holiday — busy
Shōwa Day national holiday — parks and shrines open normally; some government museums closed. Retail open. Expect more crowds at popular spots.
9:00
Meiji Jingu Shrine
Tokyo's most serene Shinto shrine, set in a forested 70-hectare park. Arrive early before Harajuku fills. The forested walk to the shrine is itself remarkable. Always open.
10:30
Harajuku / Takeshita Street
Tokyo's most chaotic teen fashion street — even if not buying, a 30-min walk through is worthwhile for the spectacle. Crepe stalls every few metres.
11:30
Omotesando — Fez shopping priority ⭐
Tokyo's equivalent of Avenue Montaigne. Louis Vuitton (Omotesando flagship, Jun Aoki building) · Prada (Herzog & de Meuron glass tower) · Chanel · Hermès · Dior — all flagship stores. Plus Omotesando Hills (mid-tier in spectacular spiral architecture). Allow 3–4 hours for serious shopping. Lunch at Anniversaire Café or Omotesando Koffee.
15:00
Shibuya Scramble Crossing + Shibuya Sky
Walk down from Omotesando to Shibuya. The world's busiest pedestrian crossing — observe from 2nd floor Starbucks (free) or Shibuya Sky observation deck (¥2,000pp, pre-book online). Shibuya Hikarie for upscale shopping.
Evening
Sukiyaki or shabu-shabu dinner
Imahan Shinjuku: one of Tokyo's great old-school sukiyaki restaurants (thinly sliced wagyu cooked table-side in sweet soy broth, dipped in raw egg). Book ahead. Or: Nabezo Shinjuku for a more casual shabu-shabu experience.
~ZAR 15,000+ (Omotesando flagship shopping day)
Tue 30 Apr · Tokyo Day 3 — Last Full Day
Tsukiji · Ginza · Teamlab Planets
Final day
7:00–9:00
Tsukiji Outer Market — breakfast
Fresh tamagoyaki (grilled egg omelette, sweet and savoury) · thick slabs of sashimi on rice · fresh sea urchin · oysters. The outer market opens from 5am. Arrive by 8am before it fills. This is not the wholesale inner market (that relocated to Toyosu) but the outer market remains excellent for food.
10:00
Ginza — last shopping priority
Ginza Six (luxury mall — Dior, Celine, Bottega, excellent basement food hall) · Matsuya Ginza · Itoya (8-storey stationery and pen megastore — Ramo) · Uniqlo 12-storey Ginza flagship (premium lines) · Wako (iconic clock tower, luxury goods). Japanese designers: Issey Miyake, Comme des Garçons, Yohji Yamamoto all have stores in this area.
Afternoon
Teamlab Planets, Toyosu ⭐
~30 min from Ginza by train. Immersive digital art in water and mirrors — walk through rooms of koi, floating flowers, and infinite reflections. One of Japan's most extraordinary contemporary experiences. Pre-book tickets well in advance — typically sells out weeks ahead. ~¥3,200pp. Allow 2–3hrs including travel.
¥3,200pp (~ZAR 600pp). Book online immediately.
